Electrochemistry

Cell potential, Nernst equation, and Gibbs energy relationships.

Cell Potential

E°cell = E°cathode − E°anode

E°cell = (RT / nF) ln K

E°cell = (0.0257 V / n) ln K = (0.0592 V / n) log K (at 298.15 K)

Nernst Equation

Ecell = E°cell − (RT / nF) ln Q

Ecell = E°cell − (0.0592 V / n) log Q (at 298.15 K)

Gibbs Energy and Work

ΔG = −nFEcell

ΔG° = −nFE°cell

welc = wmax = −nFEcell

Q = I × t = n × F

Thermodynamics

Entropy, Gibbs free energy, and enthalpy formulas.

Entropy

ΔS = qrev / T

S = k ln W

ΔS = k ln (Wf / Wi)

ΔS° = Σ νS°(products) − Σ νS°(reactants)

ΔS = q / T

ΔSuniv = ΔSsys + ΔSsurr

ΔSuniv = ΔSsys + ΔSsurr = ΔSsys + qsurr / T

Gibbs Free Energy

ΔG = ΔH − TΔS

Enthalpy

q = c × m × ΔT = c × m × (Tfinal − Tinitial)

ΔU = q + w

ΔH°reaction = Σ n × ΔH°f(products) − Σ n × ΔH°f(reactants)

Equilibrium

Equilibrium constants and solubility product expressions.

Equilibrium Constants

For mA + nB ⇌ xC + yD:

Qc = ([C]x[D]y) / ([A]m[B]n)

QP = (PCxPDy) / (PAmPBn)

P = MRT

Kc = Qc at equilibrium

Kp = Qp at equilibrium

KP = Kc(RT)Δn

Solubility Product

MpXq(s) ⇌ pMm+(aq) + qXn−(aq)

Ksp = [Mm+]p[Xn−]q

Acid-Base Chemistry

Water equilibrium, acid/base constants, and buffer equations.

Water Equilibrium

Kw = [H3O+][OH−] = 1.0 × 10−14 (at 25°C)

pH = −log[H3O+]

pOH = −log[OH−]

[H3O+] = 10−pH

[OH−] = 10−pOH

pH + pOH = pKw = 14.00 (at 25°C)

Acid and Base Equilibria

Ka = [H3O+][A−] / [HA]

Kb = [HB+][OH−] / [B]

Ka × Kb = 1.0 × 10−14 = Kw

pKa = −log Ka

pKb = −log Kb

Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation

pH = pKa + log ([A−] / [HA])

Percent Ionization

Percent ionization = ([H3O+]eq / [HA]0) × 100

Chemical Kinetics

Reaction rates, integrated rate laws, and Arrhenius relationships.

Reaction Rates

For aA → bB: −(1 / a) Δ[A] / Δt = (1 / b) Δ[B] / Δt

Zero-Order Reactions

[A]t = −kt + [A]0 (integrated rate law)

t1/2 = [A]0 / 2k (half-life)

First-Order Reactions

ln[A]t = −kt + ln[A]0 (integrated rate law)

t1/2 = 0.693 / k (half-life)

Second-Order Reactions

1 / [A]t = kt + 1 / [A]0 (integrated rate law)

t1/2 = 1 / ([A]0k) (half-life)

Arrhenius Equation

k = Ae−Ea / RT

ln k = (−Ea / R)(1 / T) + ln A

ln (k1 / k2) = (Ea / R)(1 / T2 − 1 / T1)

Gas Laws

Ideal gas relationships, pressure, and kinetic molecular theory.

Ideal Gas Law

PV = nRT

Pressure Relationships

P = F / A

P = hρg

PTotal = PA + PB + PC + … = ΣPi

PA = XAPTotal

Mole Fraction

XA = nA / nTotal

Diffusion and Effusion

Rate of diffusion = amount of gas passing through an area / unit of time

Rate of effusion of gas A / rate of effusion of gas B = √(mB / mA) = √(MB / MA)

Kinetic Molecular Theory

urms = √u² = √((u12 + u22 + u32 + u42 + …) / n)

KEavg = (3 / 2)RT

urms = √(3RT / M)

Real Gases

Z = (molar volume at same T and P) / (ideal molar volume at same T and P) = (P × Vm / RT)measured

(P + (n2a / V2)) × (V − nb) = nRT (van der Waals equation)

Colligative Properties

Vapor pressure, boiling/freezing changes, and osmotic pressure.

Vapor Pressure

Ci = kPi (Henry's Law)

PA = XAPA* (Raoult's Law)

Psolution = ΣPi = ΣXiPi*

Psolution = XsolventP*solvent

Boiling Point Elevation and Freezing Point Depression

ΔTb = Kbm

ΔTf = Kfm

Osmotic Pressure

Π = MRT

Phase Changes & Physical Chemistry

Capillary action, Clausius-Clapeyron, and Bragg's law.

Capillary Action

h = (2T cos θ) / (rρg)

Clausius-Clapeyron Equation

P = Ae−ΔHvap / RT

ln P = (−ΔHvap / RT) + ln A

ln(P1 / P2) = (ΔHvap / R)(1 / T2 − 1 / T1)

Bragg's Law

nλ = 2d sin θ

Atomic Structure & Spectroscopy

Wave-particle duality and hydrogen atom relationships.

Wave-Particle Duality

c = λν

E = hν = hc / λ, where h = 6.626 × 10−34 J·s

Hydrogen Atom

1 / λ = R∞(1 / n12 − 1 / n22)

En = −kZ2 / n2, n = 1, 2, 3, …

ΔE = kZ2(1 / n12 − 1 / n22)

r = (n2 / Z)a0

Chemical Bonding

Bond order, formal charge, bond energy, and lattice energy.

Bond Order

Bond order = (number of bonding electrons − number of antibonding electrons) / 2

Formal Charge

Formal charge = valence electrons (free atom) − lone pair electrons − (1 / 2) bonding electrons

Bond Energy

XY(g) → X(g) + Y(g), DX−Y = ΔH°

ΔH = Σ Dbonds broken − Σ Dbonds formed

Lattice Energy

For a solid MX: MX(s) → Mn+(g) + Xn−(g), ΔHlattice

For an ionic crystal: ΔHlattice = C(Z+)(Z−) / R0

Stoichiometry & Solutions

Percent composition, formulas, molarity, and concentration units.

Percent Composition

%X = (mass X / mass compound) × 100%

Empirical and Molecular Formulas

(molecular or molar mass / empirical formula mass) (amu or g/mol) = n formula units/molecule

(AxBy)n = AnxBny

Molarity and Dilution

M = mol solute / L solution

C1V1 = C2V2

Concentration Units

Percent by mass = (mass of solute / mass of solution) × 100

ppm = (mass solute / mass solution) × 106 ppm

ppb = (mass solute / mass solution) × 109 ppb

Percent Yield

Percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100

Atomic Mass & Basic Measurements

Average atomic mass, density, and temperature conversions.

Average Atomic Mass

Average mass = Σ (fractional abundance × isotopic mass)

Density

Density = mass / volume

Temperature Conversions

T°C = (5 / 9)(T°F − 32)

T°F = (9 / 5)T°C + 32

TK = °C + 273.15

T°C = K − 273.15
